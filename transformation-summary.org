*** 2. New Components Integrated

**** Scientific Research Timeline
- *Source*: Adapted from =GerardoCendejas/org-roam-timeline=
- *Purpose*: Visual timeline for tracking research chronology
- *Features*:
  - Timeline visualization of research papers
  - Publication date tracking and sorting
  - Reading progress visualization over time
  - Filter by research tags and domains
  - Date zoom and focus capabilities
  - Follow mode for real-time updates
- *Integration*: Works with =citation-database= for paper metadata

**** Scientific Concept Tree
- *Source*: Adapted from =bradmont/org-roam-tree=
- *Purpose*: Tree-style display for concept relationships
- *Features*:
  - Hierarchical tree views of concept connections
  - Group relationships by source (files, journals, studies)
  - Expandable/collapsible branches
  - Multiple tree types (concepts, citations, evidence)
  - Integration with =concept-relationships=
- *Integration*: Uses =citation-database= for relationship data

**** Scientific YASnippet
- *Source*: Integrated from =joaotavora/yasnippet=
- *Purpose*: Template library for academic documents
- *Features*:
  - Comprehensive template library
  - Research article templates (APA, MLA, Chicago, IEEE)
  - Literature review templates
  - Conference paper templates
  - Thesis and dissertation templates
  - Automatic metadata insertion
  - Integration with document creation workflow

** Academic Writing Workflows

*** Literature Review Workflow

1. *Use Template*: =M-x yas-expand-snippet= → =literature-review=
2. *Fill Research Questions*: Define 3-5 key questions
3. *Search Strategy*: Document databases and keywords
4. *Add Papers*: =M-x scientific-document-create= for each paper
5. *Track Reading*: Set reading status for each paper
6. *Build Concept Map*: Extract concepts and create relationships
7. *Visualize*: Use =M-x scientific-research-timeline-show-node=
8. *Write Review*: Synthesize findings using template structure

*** Conference Paper Workflow

1. *Use Template*: =M-x yas-expand-snippet= → =conference-paper=
2. *Fill Abstract*: 100-200 words
3. *Add Content*: Introduction, methods, results, discussion
4. *Track Progress*: Set reading status to "writing"
5. *Cite References*: Link to papers in citation-database
6. *Add to Timeline*: =M-x scientific-research-timeline-add-date=
7. *Visualize*: Track in research timeline

*** Thesis Writing Workflow

1. *Use Template*: =M-x yas-expand-snippet= → =thesis-master=
2. *Fill in Details*: Institution, department, advisor
3. *Complete Chapters*: Follow template structure
4. *Track Progress*: Use timeline to track chapter completion
5. *Build Citation Database*: Add all sources to citation-database
6. *Create Concept Map*: Organize concepts by chapter
7. *Review with Committee*: Use visualizer to show concept network
8. *Export*: Use standard export for final submission
