✅ ICMJE Compliance:

✅ University Thesis Requirements:
  - Master's thesis: 40-80 pages, 400-word abstract
  - Doctoral dissertation: 150-300+ pages, 350-word abstract

  - Chapter structure requirements

✅ Conference Paper Standards:
  - Abstract: 100-200 words
  - Total: 3000 words
  - Focused on methodology and results

* Chronological Writing Process

The system enforces a proven academic writing workflow:

**Phase 1: Preparation and Planning**
- Set paper type and journal requirements
- Review word count limits from official sources
- Define research questions/hypotheses
- Configure page layout settings

**Phase 2: Literature Review**
- Use literature-review template
- Define search strategy (databases, keywords, time period)
- Document inclusion/exclusion criteria
- Analyze and synthesize existing studies
- Identify research gaps

**Phase 3: Methods and Design**
- Insert methods section with template
- Describe research design (quantitative, qualitative, mixed)
- Include participant/sample descriptions
- Document data collection instruments and measures
- Outline procedures step-by-step
- Include ethical considerations (IRB approval, informed consent)

**Phase 4: Results and Data Presentation**
- Present descriptive statistics
- Report inferential statistics
- Format tables with proper captions
- Create figures with caption placeholders
- Include statistical significance levels
- Present data clearly and objectively

**Phase 5: Discussion and Analysis**
- Interpret findings in context of existing literature
- Compare results with previous studies
- Discuss theoretical implications
- Address limitations honestly
- Suggest mechanisms explaining findings
- Discuss practical applications

**Phase 6: Conclusion and Implications**
- Summarize key findings without introducing new information
- State conclusions clearly and confidently
- Highlight theoretical contributions
- Highlight practical contributions
- Suggest specific future research directions
- Connect to broader literature

* Citation Formatting Standards

**Supported Styles with Formatting Functions**:
Built into scientific-page-view:

- **APA (American Psychological Association)**:
  Format: (Smith, 2020, p. 123)
  Built-in: ~(scientific-page-view-format-citation "Smith, 2020, p. 123")~

- **MLA (Modern Language Association)**:
  Format: Smith 123
  Built-in: ~(scientific-page-view-format-citation "Smith 123")~

- **Chicago (17th Edition)**:
  Format: Smith (2020) 123
  Built-in: ~(scientific-page-view-format-citation "Smith (2020) 123")~

- **IEEE**:
  Format: [1] Smith et al.
  Built-in: ~(scientific-page-view-format-citation "[1] Smith et al.")~

- **Vancouver**:
  Format: Smith et al. 2020;123:456-789
  Built-in: ~(scientific-page-view-format-citation "Smith et al. 2020;123:456-789")~

**Journal-Specific Templates**:
- PLOS: PLOS citation style, structured abstract format
- Nature: Numbered citations, 6-page limit, author information
- Science: Author information formatting
- IEEE: Bracketed citation format
- APA: Parenthetical format with year
